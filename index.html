Gender Equality

Himalayan Hope Home

by The Small World

This program intends to pay the educational and daily living expenditures of 30 underprivileged girls in the Solukhumbhu district's Himalayan region. Children in this program are either orphaned or have parents who cannot afford to support them. They have a safe place to live, an education chance, and are happy, healthy, and at peace because of the Small World.

Shattering the Shame

by Days for Girls International

Girls are quite often sequestered in cattle sheds or small huts in the remote highlands of western Nepal during their periods, as part of a cultural practice known as Chhaupadi. Girls are not only embarrassed by menstruation, but they are also frequently exposed to genuine risk, such as harsh weather, smoke inhalation, and attacks. The charge is being led by a local training squad of Nepali girls and women to stop this malpractice for good.

Girls' Education

by The Small World

The Small World's goal is to generate funds for 30 orphaned and vulnerable girls at Himalayan Hope Home to help them with their scholastic and day-to-day needs. These children will have a safe place to live, an education chance, and will be happy, healthy, and at peace thanks to your generous contribution.

Schooling and Housing for Bon Children

by Creating Hope International

The Bon are a minority ethnic group in Nepal that live primarily in rural areas. Because of insecurity and a lack of schools, only around one-third of the children attend school. This project will provide housing and food through post-secondary education to Nepalese Bon children and youth who are orphaned or underprivileged.

Support-A-Child

by Environmental Camps for Conservation Awareness

Many people's financial situation has deteriorated as a result of the COVID-19 outbreak. Aside from financial difficulties, a lack of suitable educational resources in schools and a lack of hands-on practical activities reduces students' willingness to attend school on a regular basis, and they finally drop out. ECCA will assist schools by providing educational materials that will motivate children to study harder and stay in school.

Ama Ghar Displaced Children Fund

by The Ama Foundation

The devastating 2015 earthquakes in Nepal orphaned or displaced many children. The Ama Foundation collaborated with anti-trafficking organizations and the Nepalese government to help these children find a safe haven. Since 2015, they've welcomed 30 new children into their home, which can accommodate 75 children, but they only have money to care for the 46 children who are presently residing there. Your support allows The Ama Foundation to take in additional children and provide them with a loving home, an education, and a bright future.

Child Protection

The Friends of Arhaus Children's Home

by The Small World

Children in remote Himalayan communities were severely impacted by the terrible Nepal earthquake in 2015, followed by Covid-19. The Friends of Arhaus Children's Home is the first home in the Everest region to give orphaned, abandoned, and vulnerable children with safe, high-quality, and free educational opportunities.

Protect Street Children

by Kidasha

In Pokhara, there are over 250 street children. These children are part of a larger group of youngsters who are victims of chronic endemic poverty, family conflict, domestic violence and abuse, for whom the street is a temporary or longer-term means of survival or escape. The Kidasha facility for street boys in Pokhara is the only one of its kind in the city, providing not only temporary shelter and care, but also counseling, assistance, and training to help youngsters leave the streets and rebuild their lives, either with their families or independently.

Rescue children from Slavery

by Child Rescue Nepal

Nepal has the fifth highest rate of modern slavery in the world (Walk Free Foundation 2013), with reports estimating that up to 15,000 individuals are trafficked each year, with minors accounting for a third of those trafficked. Rescue of Children Nepal wants to put an end to child trafficking in the country, and they want to achieve so by combining rescue and preventative efforts. They want to set up an emergency fund so that they can respond fast and save children in perilous situations.
